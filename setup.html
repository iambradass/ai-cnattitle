ChatGPT Setup

The all-rounder. Huge ecosystem, best voice mode, and if you're on Plus ($20/mo) or Pro ($200/mo) you get a stack of features most people never touch. The beginner section gets you from zero to functional. The advanced section is where the magic is.

Beginner
Custom Instructions

Tell ChatGPT who you are

One-time setup that loads into every conversation. Skip this and you're starting every chat from scratch.

  1. Profile icon (bottom-left) → Customize ChatGPT
  2. Paste what you just generated from Step 1 into the first box
  3. In the second box, describe your tone (direct / warm / data-driven)
  4. Hit Save
Custom GPTs

Build specialized assistants

A Custom GPT is ChatGPT with a job title, a script, and uploaded reference files. Build one and you'll stop re-explaining yourself every time.

  1. Explore GPTs in the sidebar → Create (top right)
  2. Name it after a job: "Listing Writer", "Buyer Intake Interviewer", "CMA Narrative Builder"
  3. Instructions: describe exactly what it does, what tone, what it should avoid, what the output format is
  4. Upload reference files — brand guidelines, past listing descriptions, sample CMAs, your buyer questionnaire
  5. Enable Web Browsing for current data, Code Interpreter for spreadsheets, DALL-E for image gen
Starter GPT instructions — Listing Writer 
You are a luxury listing description writer for a Fort Worth real estate agent. For every listing I share, produce:
1. A 280-character MLS remark (no fluff, lead with the #1 feature)
2. A 600-900 word full description with 3-4 subheaded sections
3. Five short social captions (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
Always highlight school district, walkability, and any feature that justifies price per sq ft. Never use the words "nestled," "charming," or "must-see."

Advanced
Projects + Memory

Make ChatGPT remember forever

Projects are workspace folders that keep conversations and reference files grouped. Memory is ChatGPT's long-term recall across all chats. Turn both on and ChatGPT becomes an actual colleague instead of a goldfish.

  1. Sidebar → + New Project → name it per client ("DavidAndLori-BuyersRelo") or workflow ("ListingLaunches2026")
  2. Drop every related doc in there — signed listing agreements, comps, photos, inspection reports
  3. Settings → Personalization → turn Memory ON; then say "Remember that I use Follow Up Boss, always sign emails 'Bradley,' and my buyer intake template lives in this Project"
  4. From now on, every conversation inside that Project auto-loads those files and your memory applies everywhere
CanvasNew

Write and edit side-by-side

Canvas opens a doc panel next to the chat so you can edit a listing description, email draft, or contract letter in real-time with ChatGPT suggesting changes inline. Way faster than copy-paste cycles.

  • Start with: "Open this in canvas so we can edit together"
  • Highlight sections to ask for tone changes, length cuts, or rewrites of just that paragraph
  • Use canvas for: full MLS descriptions, seller net sheet explanations, buyer consult agendas, lender memos
Code Interpreter / Data Analysis

Drop in a CSV. Get insights.

Export your MLS pull, your CRM list, or your lender pipeline to CSV and drop it in. ChatGPT runs Python under the hood to slice it any way you ask.

  • "Here's 90 days of sold comps in Westlake. Show me median $/sf by month with a chart."
  • "This is my Follow Up Boss contact export. Bucket them into A/B/C leads based on tags and last-contact date."
  • "Here's my closed transactions YTD. What's my average commission, best referral source, and monthly trend?"
Try this — paste with an MLS CSV export 
I'm uploading a CSV of 90 days of sold comps in [ZIP]. Produce:
1. Median $/sf per month with a line chart
2. Avg days-on-market by price band ($200-400k, $400-600k, $600k+)
3. Top 3 subdivisions by closed volume
4. A one-paragraph market narrative I could send to a seller considering listing next month
Voice Mode + Mobile App

Your drivetime AI

Advanced Voice on mobile is genuinely conversational — no robotic back-and-forth. Use it between appointments.

  • Post-showing debrief: "I just showed 1245 Cedar Lane to the Davises. They loved the kitchen, hated the backyard. Draft my follow-up email."
  • Pre-listing prep: "Quiz me on the objections a seller in this price range might raise, one at a time."
  • Drive-time CMA: "Give me three comps for a 2,400 sqft ranch in Fort Worth 76109, most recent first."
Screen Sharing + Vision

Show ChatGPT exactly what you're looking at

The desktop app can see your screen. The mobile camera can see a house.

  • Desktop: pull up an MLS listing → "Rewrite this description in my brand voice"
  • Mobile: walk through a listing → "Dictate a property walkthrough for my MLS remarks as I film this"
  • Inspection: "Here's page 4 of the inspection report — summarize the seller-responsibility items in plain language for my buyer"
Agents & TasksNew

Scheduled and agentic actions

Tasks (Plus+) let ChatGPT run on a schedule. Agents (Pro) let it take actions in a browser on your behalf.

  • Task: "Every Monday at 7am, check my market area for new listings under $500k with 4+ beds. Email me a shortlist."
  • Agent: "Go to my MLS, pull the new listings report for my hotlist areas, download the PDFs to my Drive" (Pro plan)
  • Task: "Every Friday, summarize my week's client emails into follow-up priorities"
DALL-E Image Generation

Marketing visuals without Canva

  • "Create a 'Just Listed' Instagram post for 1245 Cedar Lane — modern farmhouse, 4/3, pool. Brand color #faed36."
  • "Design a sold celebration graphic with 'JUST SOLD IN 5 DAYS' and a gold accent on dark navy"
  • "Generate a neighborhood lifestyle collage for a Keller, TX listing — parks, schools, dining"
Pro Move

Build a "Daily Market Brief" Custom GPT with your market ZIP codes saved, your preferred sources, and your output format. Open it once every morning with "brief me" — you'll have a real-estate-specific market rundown before your first coffee.

Claude Setup

The deep thinker. Best writing quality, best at long contracts, and Artifacts quietly turns it into a tool-building platform. If ChatGPT is the all-rounder, Claude is the one you hand the 45-page title commitment to.

Beginner
User Preferences

Tell Claude who you are

  1. Profile icon → Settings
  2. Profile → find What personal preferences should Claude consider
  3. Paste your generated instructions from Step 1
  4. Save
Projects

Permanent context with a knowledge base

Projects are workspaces with their own uploaded documents. Claude reads every file in the Project for every conversation you have inside it.

  1. Sidebar → Projects → Create Project
  2. Name it for the workflow: "Listing Launches", "Buyer Consults", "CMAs"
  3. Project Instructions: describe exactly what Claude should do inside this workspace
  4. Upload files: templates, brand guide, buyer intake form, past award-winning listing descriptions

ArtifactsNew

Claude builds tools for you

Artifacts are working mini-apps Claude generates inside the chat — calculators, forms, charts, HTML pages. For real estate this is absurdly useful.

  • "Build me a mortgage payment calculator I can embed on my listing page — 30-year fixed, include taxes and insurance fields, dark theme"
  • "Create an interactive buyer intake form that collects budget, timing, must-haves, and deal-breakers, then generates a summary I can save"
  • "Build a net-to-seller calculator that factors in commission, title fees, taxes, and concessions. Clean mobile-friendly design."
  • "Design a listing flyer as an HTML page with my photo, stats, description, and a QR code placeholder"
Extended Thinking

When you need the deep dive

Toggle Extended Thinking (top of chat) and Claude takes longer but thinks harder. Use this for the hard stuff where getting it right the first time matters.

  • Analyzing a complicated contract or title commitment
  • Working through a multi-party negotiation strategy
  • Structuring a complex deal (1031, seller financing, leaseback)
  • Writing a high-stakes client email that needs to land perfectly
PDF & Document Analysis

Drop in a 40-page doc. Get answers.

Claude handles long documents better than anything else on the market. The 200K-token context window eats entire transaction files.

  • Drag in a title commitment → "Flag every exception that could kill my buyer's deal and draft a plain-language cheat sheet"
  • Drop in an inspection report → "Categorize every item into cosmetic / maintenance / material defect, and draft my repair-request letter"
  • Paste an HOA packet → "Summarize the restrictions that would matter to a buyer with two dogs and an RV"
  • Upload an appraisal → "Explain the adjustments to a first-time buyer in 3 bullet points"
SkillsNew

Automated capability packs

Claude Skills are reusable playbooks Claude runs on command. For agents/lenders, think of them as macros: "run-listing-launch", "build-cma", "draft-seller-disclosure-email".

  • Use prebuilt skills from the library (doc processing, PDF filling, spreadsheet analysis)
  • Build your own: tell Claude "save this process as a skill called listing-launch" and it packages your workflow for reuse
Desktop App + Shortcut

Summon Claude from anywhere

Install the desktop app and set the keyboard shortcut. Now Claude is one keystroke away from wherever you're working — inside your email, your CRM, your MLS.

  • Writing a reply in Gmail? Hit the shortcut, dictate the intent, paste Claude's draft back in.
  • Reading a contract PDF? Hit the shortcut, paste the tough clause, get a plain-language explanation.
  • In MLS? Hit the shortcut, paste the raw listing info, get MLS remarks in your voice.
Pro Move

Build a "Deal Docs" Project for every active transaction. Upload the contract, amendments, inspection, title commitment, and appraisal as they come in. When any question comes up during the deal, ask Claude inside that Project — it has the entire file and answers with specifics, not generics.

Gemini Setup

The Google-native AI. If your life already runs on Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs, and Sheets, Gemini is a superpower because it reads and writes directly inside those apps. Plus a 2M-token context window no one else matches.

  3. Write detailed instructions — purpose, tone, output format
  4. Save — it appears in your sidebar, one click to use

Advanced
Deep Research

Agentic research reports

Deep Research is a whole different mode. Ask a big question, Gemini builds a research plan, scans dozens of sources, and delivers a structured report with citations. It takes minutes — but the output is genuinely impressive.

  • "Deep research: What are the top 10 relocation factors for tech workers moving from the Bay Area to DFW? Include salary adjustments, commute patterns, and school priorities."
  • "Deep research: Compare the 4 largest master-planned communities in Westlake TX by HOA fees, amenities, school ratings, and median sale price."
  • "Deep research: Current rate forecasts from the 5 major mortgage banks — what's consensus through Q4?"
Gemini in Gmail, Docs, Sheets

AI in-place, not in a tab

On Google Workspace, Gemini lives inside the apps. No tab-switching.

  • Gmail: "Help me write" in your voice, summarize 20-email threads, draft follow-ups from a single highlight
  • Docs: generate listing presentations, rewrite contract addendum explanations in plain language, build seller net sheets
  • Sheets: "Build a formula that flags any lead in this list that hasn't been contacted in 30 days" — it writes the formula for you
NotebookLMNew

The secret weapon for listings & client prep

NotebookLM (at notebooklm.google.com) is a Google research tool that turns your documents into an interactive knowledge base — complete with an AI-generated podcast of two voices discussing your content. Sounds gimmicky. It's not.

  • Upload neighborhood guides, school reports, and market data → generate a 15-min podcast your client listens to during their drive to the showing
  • Upload a listing's CMA, HOA docs, and inspection → get an instant Q&A interface + a podcast walkthrough
  • Upload training materials → ask specific questions and get cited answers
2M Token Context

Feed it the whole file cabinet

Gemini's context window is massive. You can paste an entire year's worth of closed transactions, every page of a 300-page HOA packet, or 50 comparable listings in one prompt.

  • "Here's every listing I took in 2025. Analyze which price points, photo counts, and descriptions had the best DOM-to-list-price ratio."
  • "Here's my full lender ratesheet history for 24 months. Build me a pattern analysis of when rates moved and what triggered each move."
Gemini Live

Real-time voice + camera

On mobile, point your camera at anything and have a real conversation about what you're seeing.

  • Walking a listing: "What are the red flags in this crawl space?"
  • In a client meeting: "Live transcribe and flag any objections I should address"
  • At an open house: "Keep a running log of which features visitors comment on"
Pro Move

Run NotebookLM on every listing you take. Upload the CMA, HOA docs, seller disclosures, and a neighborhood brief. Share the notebook link with your seller — they get an interactive Q&A tool about their own listing, and you look like an actual wizard.

Perplexity Setup

The research engine. Different species from the other three. Sourced answers with current data — perfect for pre-meeting prep, market intelligence, and anything where citations matter. If the question is "what's true right now," this is the tool.

Beginner
AI Profile

Tell Perplexity who you are

  1. Profile icon → Settings
  2. AI Profile → paste your generated instructions from Step 1
  3. Save
Spaces

Topic-based research workspaces

  1. Sidebar → Spaces → Create Space
  2. Name it: "Southlake Market", "Luxury Relocation", "Commercial Retail"
  3. Upload files or add notes to seed the Space with context
  4. Every search inside the Space builds on everything already there
Pro Search

More than a search engine

Pro Search pulls from multiple current sources and synthesizes a sourced answer. This is how you walk into a listing appointment knowing more about the neighborhood than the people who live there.

  1. Toggle Pro on before the search
  2. Ask a specific question, not a keyword: "What are the top 5 concerns for Bay Area families relocating to Frisco TX, with citations to current data?"
  3. Every claim comes with a source — click through to verify before you quote to a client
Advanced
Labs

Full research reports with charts

Perplexity Labs (Pro plan) doesn't just give you a text answer — it generates fully formatted reports with charts, tables, and dashboards. Drop these straight into a listing presentation or buyer consult.

  • "Build me a relocation report for a family moving from Austin to Keller. Include cost-of-living comparison, school ratings, commute analysis, and the top 3 neighborhoods with charts."
  • "Build a luxury market report for $1M+ homes in Southlake — 12-month trend, median DOM, top 5 builders, price-per-sqft comparison to 76092."
Comet BrowserNew

AI that browses for you

Comet is Perplexity's AI-native browser. Ask it to do something online and it does it — no clicks, no tabs. Still early, but for research tasks this saves hours.

  • "Go to my MLS portal and pull every new listing today in 76109 under $600k. Save as PDF."
  • "Check the Redfin, Zillow, and Realtor.com estimates for 1245 Cedar Lane. Email me the three numbers side-by-side."
  • "Research the top 5 schools in Westlake and build me a one-pager with ratings, demographics, and highlights"
File Upload

Research on your own documents

  • Upload a buyer's wishlist PDF → "Match this wishlist to 20 current listings in my market with citations"
  • Upload a lender's ratesheet → "Compare this to current market rates and tell me what's competitive"
  • Upload an HOA packet → "Pull the 10 restrictions most likely to be a deal-breaker for a buyer with kids, dogs, and home business"
Pages

Publish researched content

Pages turns any Perplexity research into a shareable, published webpage. Drop the link in a client email and skip the attachments.

  • Turn a Deep Research into a published neighborhood guide you share with your sphere
  • Build a luxury market update Page and link it from your email signature every Monday
  • Create buyer-facing "Why Fort Worth?" relocation Pages and send the link during consults
Pro Move

Before every listing appointment, run a Perplexity Pro search on: the neighborhood's last-12-month trend, the 3 closest competing listings, recent local news that could affect value, and the seller's "why they're moving" rationale. You walk in with sourced data the seller didn't know about their own block. Credibility, instant.
