ChatGPT Setup Guide

ChatGPT is the most widely used AI tool on the market. If you're on a Plus or Pro plan, the features below will turn it from a chat window into something that actually knows your business.

Custom Instructions

Tell ChatGPT Who You Are

Custom instructions are background context that ChatGPT reads before every single conversation. Set them once and you never have to re-explain yourself.

  1. Open ChatGPT, click your profile icon in the bottom-left, then Customize ChatGPT
  2. In the first box ("What would you like ChatGPT to know about you?"), write a paragraph about who you are: your name, your role (agent, lender, broker), what market you serve, what types of clients you typically work with, your brokerage name, and how long you've been in the business
  3. In the second box ("How would you like ChatGPT to respond?"), describe your communication style: do you want things casual or formal? Short and direct or detailed? Should it avoid jargon, use your local market terminology, etc.
  4. Hit Save. Every new conversation from this point forward starts with this context loaded
Custom GPTs

Build Purpose-Built Assistants

Custom GPTs are specialized versions of ChatGPT that you configure for a specific job. Think of it like creating a team member who only does one thing but does it well.

  1. Click Explore GPTs in the sidebar, then Create in the top right
  2. Give it a name and description that matches its purpose (e.g., "Listing Research Assistant" or "Client Follow-Up Writer")
  3. In the Instructions field, be specific about what it does, what tone it uses, what it should avoid, and what kind of output you expect
  4. Upload any reference files it should know about: your brand guidelines, market data, templates, or past examples of work you liked
  5. Enable Web Browsing if it needs current data. Enable Code Interpreter if it needs to analyze spreadsheets or data

Claude Setup Guide

Claude's strength is deep, thoughtful work. Projects let you load context that stays active across every conversation in that workspace, and MCP connectors give it direct access to your tools.

User Preferences

Tell Claude Who You Are

User Preferences work like custom instructions. Claude reads them at the start of every conversation so it always has context about you and your work.

  1. Click your profile icon, then go to Settings
  2. Find Profile and look for the User Preferences section
  3. Write a paragraph covering: your name, your role, your market area, your client types, your communication style, and anything you want Claude to always keep in mind
  4. Hit Save. This context is now loaded into every new conversation automatically
Projects

Give Claude Permanent Context

Projects are workspaces where you upload reference documents that Claude can access in every conversation within that project. Market reports, brand guidelines, transaction checklists, client profiles. Load it once, use it forever.

  1. In the sidebar, click Projects, then Create Project
  2. Give it a clear name like "Listing Research" or "Client Communications"
  3. Add Project Instructions that tell Claude the purpose of this workspace and how it should behave when you're working in it
  4. Upload relevant files: templates, past examples, market data, brand voice guidelines, anything Claude should reference
  5. Start a new conversation inside the project. Claude now has access to everything you uploaded, every time
Recommended Connections

Connect Your Workflow

Claude uses MCP (Model Context Protocol) connectors to talk directly to your apps. Once connected, it can read your emails, check your calendar, search your files, and more without leaving the conversation.

Pro Tip

Claude also has a desktop app and mobile app. The desktop app has a keyboard shortcut that summons Claude from any screen on your computer. Use it mid-workflow when you need a quick answer without switching tabs.

Gemini Setup Guide

If you already live in Google Workspace, Gemini is the lowest-friction option. Turn on Extensions and it plugs directly into Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Docs, and Sheets. No copy-pasting required.

Custom Instructions

Tell Gemini Who You Are

Gemini lets you set instructions that shape how it responds in every conversation. Same concept as the other tools: give it context once, benefit every time.

  1. Open gemini.google.com and click Settings (gear icon)
  2. Find Saved Info and click into it
  3. Add details about yourself: name, profession, market area, client types, communication preferences
  4. Save your changes. Gemini now factors this into every response
Gems

Build Custom AI Agents

Gems are Gemini's version of custom assistants. You define a persona, give it instructions, and it becomes a dedicated tool for a specific task.

  1. In the Gemini sidebar, click Gem manager, then New Gem
  2. Name it something specific to its job (e.g., "Market Report Writer" or "Open House Follow-Up")
  3. Write detailed instructions about what it does, the tone it uses, and what output format you expect
  4. Save it. Your Gem appears in the sidebar and is ready to use anytime
Extensions

Connect Google Workspace

This is where Gemini really shines if you're already in the Google ecosystem. Extensions let Gemini read and interact with your Google apps directly.

Perplexity Setup Guide

Perplexity fills a different role than the other three. It's a research tool first. Sourced answers, current data, deep dives. The play here is pre-meeting prep and market intelligence.

Profile

Tell Perplexity Who You Are

Perplexity lets you set a profile that shapes its responses. Less customizable than the others, but still worth setting up so it knows your context.

  1. Open Perplexity, click your profile icon, then Settings
  2. Find the Profile or AI Profile section
  3. Fill in your name, profession, location, and areas of interest. Mention your market area and the types of properties or clients you work with
  4. Save. Perplexity will tailor search results and summaries to your professional context
Spaces

Organize Your Research

Spaces let you keep ongoing research organized by topic. Instead of starting from scratch every time, you build a knowledge base that grows over time.

  1. In the sidebar, click Spaces, then Create Space
  2. Name it for the topic you're researching (e.g., "Southlake Market Trends" or "Luxury Buyer Relocation")
  3. Add relevant files, links, or notes to give the Space context
  4. Every search you run inside that Space builds on everything that's already there
Pro Search

Go Deeper Than Google

Pro Search doesn't just find links. It reads multiple sources, synthesizes the information, and gives you a sourced answer with citations. Perfect for client prep.

  1. Toggle Pro on before running a search (it's the button next to the search bar)
  2. Ask specific questions: "What are the top 5 concerns for Bay Area families relocating to Frisco, TX?" or "What's the average days on market for homes over $1.5M in Westlake?"
  3. Perplexity will pull from multiple current sources and cite each one. You can click through to verify anything
Use Case

Before a listing appointment or buyer meeting, run a Pro Search on the neighborhood, the buyer's current city, recent comparable sales, and local development news. Perplexity gives you sourced, current data you can reference in your meeting. This is the kind of preparation that makes clients feel like you've done your homework, because you actually did.
